# Content Tier Policy

Generated at: 2026-05-23T13:08:34Z

Compute Current now uses a three-tier public publishing model.

## Tier 1: Longform Analysis

Use when relevance is high, extraction is clean, evidence is deep enough, and the item has clear infrastructure implications.

Public behavior:
- Homepage card
- Full article page
- Sitemap entry
- RSS eligibility when public copy is clean
- CTA: Read analysis

Quality rules:
- At least 4,500 visible body characters
- At least 5 source-backed facts
- At least 6 meaningful paragraphs
- At least 4 section headings
- Includes second-order implication, limitation, and decision implication

## Tier 2: Editorial Brief Or Signal Card

Use when the item is relevant and useful but does not merit a full local article, or when source evidence is too thin for longform but safe enough to summarize as a card.

Public behavior:
- Homepage card
- Archive card
- Source CTA when no detail page is produced
- No local full article

Quality rules:
- Card copy must read like editorial copy.
- The card must include a title, source, date, category, label, deck, and CTA.
- The card must not explain why it did or did not qualify for longform.

## Tier 3: Hidden

Use when the item is irrelevant, consumer-tech oriented, spammy, clipped, boilerplate-heavy, extraction-failed, or outside the Compute Current product boundary.

Public behavior:
- No homepage card
- No public archive card
- No sitemap entry
- No RSS item
- No public local article
